Inventory Management Standard Operating Procedure

Purpose

The purpose of this SOP is to ensure that inventory levels are accurately maintained, recorded, and reconciled to support operational efficiency and financial accuracy.

Scope

This procedure applies to all inventory items within the company, including raw materials, work-in-progress, and finished goods.

Responsibilities

Inventory Manager: Oversees inventory counts, reconciliations, and reporting.
Finance Department: Reviews inventory reports, records adjustments, and integrates inventory data with financial reporting.

Procedure

  1. Conduct regular inventory counts (e.g., monthly, quarterly) to verify physical stock.
  2. Compare the results of the physical counts to the inventory records in the accounting software.
  3. Investigate and reconcile any discrepancies immediately to ensure accuracy.
  4. Adjust inventory records to reflect the actual stock levels as determined by the physical counts.
  5. Prepare and review inventory reports, highlighting any changes, discrepancies, or areas of concern.
  6. Finance Department to integrate adjusted inventory data into financial reports for accurate financial representation.

Documentation

Maintain records of all inventory counts, reconciliation reports, and adjustments. These documents should be stored securely for at least five years for auditing purposes and operational analysis.

Review and Update

This SOP should be reviewed at least annually to incorporate changes in inventory management practices, technology, or regulatory requirements. Updates should be documented and communicated to all relevant staff.
